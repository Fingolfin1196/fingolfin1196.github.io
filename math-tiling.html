Here is an interesting small math problem with a basis in real life that I spent some time thinking about a few days ago: How can I divide my monitor into a grid containing a user-defined number of tiles
optimally
?
Before trying to find a good optimality condition, it makes sense to formulate this problem formally:
Let $W, H \in \mathbb{N}$ be the height and width of the monitor and $T \in \mathbb{N}$ the minimum number of tiles that our grid needs to contain. A solution to the tiling problem is then a pair $\left\langle w, h \right\rangle \in \mathbb{N}^2$ for which $w \cdot h \geq T$ holds. This, however, can be simplified by noting that the minimum value of one of these variables can be calculated trivially from that of the other: If, for instance, the value for $h$ is fixed, the lowest value for $w$ that fulfils the aforementioned condition is $w ≔ \left\lceil \frac{T}{h} \right\rceil$, since the following holds: $$ w \cdot h = \left\lceil \frac{T}{h} \right\rceil \cdot h \geq \frac{T}{h} \cdot h = T $$ Since $\left\lceil \frac{T}{h} \right\rceil$ is the smallest integer for which this inequality holds, it suffices to consider either $h$ or $w$ (just swap $h$ and $w$ above) as the variable and calculate the other as above.
